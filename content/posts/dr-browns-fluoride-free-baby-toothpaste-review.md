## What Makes This Toothpaste Fluoride-Free — and Why That Matters

<img src="https://m.media-amazon.com/images/I/91bYYLiTZYL._SL1500_.jpg" alt="Dr. Brown's Baby Toothpaste ingredients and fluoride-free formula" style="width:100%;max-width:700px;display:block;margin:24px auto;border-radius:12px" />

The **American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry (AAPD)** recommends fluoride toothpaste for children starting at age 3, in a pea-sized amount. For children under 3, they recommend only a **smear** of toothpaste — and because babies and toddlers inevitably swallow most of what goes in their mouths, many pediatric dentists advise using fluoride-free formulas for the 0–3 age range to eliminate any risk of fluoride ingestion.

Dr. Brown's Fluoride-Free Toothpaste is designed exactly for this guidance. It cleans baby teeth effectively without any fluoride, meaning:

- **Zero risk of fluorosis** — a cosmetic condition caused by excess fluoride ingestion during tooth development
- **Safe if swallowed** — because toddlers will swallow it, and that's expected
- **No foaming agents** — SLS (sodium lauryl sulfate) found in most adult toothpastes can be harsh on soft infant gum tissue
- **No artificial colors** — the formula is free from synthetic dyes that have no purpose in infant oral care

This is exactly the kind of formula that pediatric dentists recommend for the earliest stage of brushing.

## How to Use Dr. Brown's Baby Toothpaste

**For infants (first tooth to 12 months):**
Apply a thin smear — about the size of a grain of rice — to a soft infant finger brush or the smallest soft-bristle toothbrush. Gently brush all tooth surfaces and along the gum line twice daily.

**For toddlers (12 months to 3 years):**
Continue using a rice grain-sized smear. At this stage, you can introduce a small toddler toothbrush with a soft head. Brush for two minutes, twice daily. Don't worry about rinsing — the fluoride-free formula is safe to swallow.

The key habit to build in this window isn't technique — it's consistency. A baby who brushes twice a day with Dr. Brown's Fluoride-Free Toothpaste is building the neural pathway that makes brushing feel like a normal, non-negotiable part of the day.
