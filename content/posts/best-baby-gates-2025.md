## Best Baby Gates 2025: Keep Your Crawler Safe Without Losing Your Mind

The moment your baby starts pulling up and cruising furniture, every staircase in your home becomes a hazard. Baby gates are the single most effective intervention for preventing fall-related injuries — the leading cause of emergency room visits for children under five. But not all gates are created equal, and the wrong gate in the wrong location can create a false sense of security.

We tested and evaluated the top baby gates of 2025 for sturdiness, ease of use, installation type, and value. Here are our top picks.

## Types of Baby Gates: Hardware vs Pressure Mounted

Before picking a gate, understand the two main installation types:

**Hardware-mounted gates** screw directly into wall studs or banisters. They are significantly more secure and are the only type approved for use at the top of stairs. Yes, they require a few holes in the wall — but a child falling down a flight of stairs is considerably worse.

**Pressure-mounted gates** use tension against the door frame or wall to stay in place. They are quick to install and leave no holes, making them ideal for doorways and bottom-of-stairs applications. Never use a pressure-mounted gate at the top of stairs.

## Safety Standards to Look For

Look for gates certified to ASTM F1004 standards (the US standard for expandable gates and enclosures) or EN1930 (the European equivalent). The Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association (JPMA) seal indicates third-party testing.

Avoid accordion-style gates with large diamond-shaped openings — these are an entrapment and strangulation hazard and have been largely phased out, but still appear secondhand.

## Installation Tips

**For hardware-mounted gates:** Always mount into studs, not just drywall. Use the mounting cup hardware if attaching to a banister to avoid crushing the wood. Check that the gate is level after installation.

**For pressure-mounted gates:** Tighten until the gate is completely immovable by an adult pushing firmly. Re-check monthly, as pressure can loosen over time.

**Height:** Make sure your gate is at least three-quarters of your child's height. A gate your toddler can reach over is not doing its job.

## When to Remove Baby Gates

Gates are most critical from 6 months (when babies begin pulling up) through about 24 months. By age 2 to 3, most children can navigate stairs safely with supervision. Teach stair safety actively — sit at the top of the stairs with your toddler and practice going down backward on hands and knees. Gates are a backup, not a substitute for teaching safe habits.
