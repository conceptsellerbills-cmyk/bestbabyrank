# Best Baby Formula 2025: Newborn, Sensitive, and Organic Options Compared

Baby formula is one of the most scrutinized product categories in existence — and for good reason. When you're feeding your infant something every 2–3 hours, 365 days a year, you want to be absolutely sure you're giving them the best possible nutrition. Whether you're formula-feeding from birth, supplementing breastfeeding, or transitioning from breast milk, this guide covers the best baby formulas of 2025 across every major category.

## A Note on Formula Choice

First and foremost: no matter what you see on social media or hear from well-meaning relatives, all infant formulas sold in the United States are required by the FDA to meet strict nutritional standards. This means that any FDA-regulated formula you buy at a store is nutritionally adequate for your baby. The differences between formulas lie in ingredients, protein sources, DHA/ARA content, added prebiotics and probiotics, and how easy they are for certain babies to digest — not in fundamental safety or nutritional completeness.

That said, some babies genuinely do better on certain formulas than others. Digestive discomfort, gas, constipation, and eczema can sometimes be improved by switching formulas. Always consult your pediatrician before changing your baby's formula.

## What to Look for in Baby Formula

**Protein source:** Most standard formulas use intact cow's milk proteins (whey and casein). Partially hydrolyzed formulas break these proteins into smaller pieces, which some sensitive babies digest more easily. Extensively hydrolyzed formulas are used for babies with allergies. Soy formulas use plant protein and are appropriate for some situations, but should only be used when recommended by a doctor.

**DHA and ARA:** These omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids support brain and eye development. They're found in breast milk and are added to most modern formulas. Look for formulas that include both.

**Prebiotics and probiotics:** Some formulas add prebiotics (like GOS or FOS) to support the gut microbiome. Research on formula-added probiotics is still evolving, but some parents report digestive benefits.

**Iron content:** All standard infant formulas should be iron-fortified (typically 12 mg per liter). Low-iron formulas are not recommended by the AAP.

**Lactose vs. non-lactose:** True lactose intolerance in infants is extremely rare (it's often confused with milk protein sensitivity). Unless your doctor has diagnosed lactose intolerance, standard lactose-based formula is appropriate.

## Best Overall Baby Formula: Similac 360 Total Care

Similac's 360 Total Care is our top pick for 2025 because it checks every box for what modern formula science considers important: 5 HMO prebiotics (human milk oligosaccharides, found naturally in breast milk), DHA, ARA, lutein, vitamin E, and no artificial growth hormones in the milk supply.

The HMO content is what sets 360 Total Care apart from older Similac formulas. HMOs are the third most abundant solid component of breast milk after fat and lactose, and research suggests they support immune development and gut health. Similac now includes five different HMOs — matching the diversity found in breast milk more closely than most competitors.

It's available in powder, ready-to-feed, and concentrate forms, and in multiple varieties including sensitive, spit-up, and supplementation versions. At around $28–32 for a 30 oz can, it's competitively priced for a premium formula.

**Pros:** 5 HMOs, strong nutritional profile, multiple formats, wide availability
**Cons:** Some babies prefer other protein profiles, slightly pricey

## Best for Sensitive Stomachs: Enfamil NeuroPro Gentlease

If your baby shows signs of gas, fussiness, or mild digestive discomfort on standard formula, Enfamil's NeuroPro Gentlease is our recommendation. It uses partially hydrolyzed proteins (meaning the proteins are partially broken down before feeding) that are easier for some sensitive digestive systems to process.

Despite the protein modification, it still delivers Enfamil's MFGM (milk fat globule membrane) and DHA — the components associated with brain development that Enfamil emphasizes in their NeuroPro line. It also contains 2'-FL HMO for immune support.

Gentlease has a good track record of reducing gas and fussiness within 24 hours of switching — though every baby is different and results vary. At a similar price point to Similac 360 Total Care, it's an excellent option for parents whose babies struggle with standard formula.

**Pros:** Partially hydrolyzed proteins, brain development nutrients, 2'-FL HMO, widely available
**Cons:** Partially hydrolyzed proteins have a slightly different taste some babies reject initially

## Best Organic Formula: Earth's Best Organic

For parents who prioritize organic ingredients, Earth's Best Organic Infant Formula is the most established and widely available option in the US market. It uses organic lactose as the carbohydrate source, organic skim milk and whey protein, and is free from artificial colors, flavors, and growth hormones.

It contains DHA and ARA from natural sources, meets all FDA nutritional requirements, and is reasonably priced compared to premium organic competitors (typically $25–30 for a 23.2 oz can). It's also more widely available at mainstream grocery stores than many organic alternatives.

**Pros:** Certified organic, no artificial additives, affordable among organic options, widely available
**Cons:** Fewer HMOs than premium conventional formulas, smaller can size

## Best European-Style Formula: HiPP Dutch Stage 1

HiPP is a German formula brand with decades of history and an exceptionally loyal following among parents who seek European-standard formulas. HiPP Dutch Stage 1 (for birth through 6 months) is certified organic, uses lactose as the primary carbohydrate (no corn syrup solids), includes prebiotics from natural milk, and uses whey-dominant protein similar to breast milk's protein ratio.

HiPP formulas have stricter organic certification requirements than USDA Organic in some areas, which appeals to parents who want the highest ingredient standards. The downside is that HiPP must be imported and is not FDA-registered — parents buy it through specialty retailers who import it. This is a grey market in the US, though the formula itself is safe and used by millions of babies in Europe.

**Pros:** High-quality organic ingredients, lactose-only carbohydrate, European regulatory standards
**Cons:** Not FDA-registered, imported (higher price and availability issues), grey market concerns

## Best Budget Formula: Kirkland Signature (Costco)

Kirkland Signature Infant Formula, sold at Costco, is manufactured by Perrigo — the same company that makes many store-brand formulas. It meets all FDA nutritional requirements, contains DHA and ARA, and costs roughly 30–40% less than name brands like Similac or Enfamil.

If your baby does well on standard formula and you don't need organic certification or premium ingredients, Kirkland is an excellent choice. The cost savings over a year of formula feeding can amount to hundreds of dollars.

**Pros:** Very affordable, meets all FDA requirements, DHA and ARA included
**Cons:** Fewer added ingredients (fewer HMOs, no prebiotics), requires Costco membership

## Formula Shortage Preparedness

The 2022 US formula shortage revealed how fragile supply chains can be. To protect yourself:

- Don't stockpile excessively, but keeping 1–2 weeks of extra supply is reasonable
- Register with your preferred brand's loyalty program for alerts
- Know one or two alternative formulas that your baby can tolerate (ask your pediatrician)
- Store powdered formula in a cool, dry place and observe expiration dates

## When to Switch Formulas

Signs that your baby may not be tolerating their current formula include: persistent blood in stool (see doctor immediately), severe vomiting, poor weight gain, or severe eczema potentially linked to dairy protein allergy. Mild gas, fussiness, and loose stools are often normal in early infancy and don't necessarily indicate a formula problem.

If you're considering switching, discuss it with your pediatrician first. Random switching between multiple formulas can actually increase digestive disruption rather than improve it.

## Final Recommendation

For most healthy full-term infants, **Similac 360 Total Care** or **Enfamil NeuroPro** are our top picks, with excellent nutritional profiles and wide availability. For sensitive babies, **Enfamil NeuroPro Gentlease** is a proven solution. Organic-preferring parents should start with **Earth's Best Organic**. And if budget is your primary concern, **Kirkland Signature** delivers reliable nutrition at the lowest cost.

Whatever formula you choose, the most important thing is that your baby is eating well, gaining weight appropriately, and that you're feeling confident and supported in your feeding choices.
