# Best Baby Bathtub 2025: Safe and Easy Options for Newborns and Infants

Bathing a newborn for the first time is intimidating. Babies are slippery when wet, cannot hold their heads up, and most do not enjoy the experience at first. The right baby bathtub makes bath time safer, easier, and eventually something your baby looks forward to. Here are the best baby bathtubs of 2025.

## When Can You Start Bathing a Newborn?

Until the umbilical cord stump falls off (usually 1 to 3 weeks after birth), only sponge baths are recommended. Once the stump has fully separated and the area has healed, you can begin full baths.

For the first several months, short, warm baths 2 to 3 times per week are sufficient. Daily baths are not necessary and can dry out sensitive newborn skin.

## What to Look for in a Baby Bathtub

**Size and stages**: Many bathtubs include newborn inserts or slings for the earliest months when babies cannot sit. Look for a tub that grows with your baby through at least 12 months.

**Safety**: A non-slip surface on the tub bottom is essential. Newborn inserts should keep the head and body elevated and secure.

**Drain plug**: Makes emptying the tub easy. Without one, you have to carry a full tub to the drain.

**Portability and storage**: Some families bathe in the kitchen sink (excellent for newborns — perfect height), while others use the bathtub. Foldable designs store easily.

**Ease of cleaning**: Mildew can develop in any water-holding product. Simple shapes without crevices dry faster and clean more easily.

## Bath Time Safety

**Water temperature**: Test with your elbow or wrist — it should feel warm, not hot. Ideal temperature is 98 to 100 degrees Fahrenheit (37 to 38 Celsius). Baby thermometers designed for bath water remove guesswork.

**Never leave baby unattended**: Infants can drown in less than 2 inches of water. Never step away, even for a second. If the phone rings or the doorbell rings, take the baby with you.

**Prepare everything in advance**: Towel, fresh diaper, clean clothes — have everything within arm's reach before you start so you never need to step away.

**Drain water before lifting baby out**: Drain the tub before removing baby to reduce the risk of dropping a slippery, wet infant into water.

**Cord stump care**: Keep the umbilical stump dry until it falls off. Sponge baths only until fully healed.

## How Often Should You Bathe a Baby?

Newborns and young babies do not need daily baths. 2 to 3 times per week is appropriate. Daily bathing can strip natural oils and cause dry skin.

Clean the diaper area at every change. Wipe face, neck folds, and hands daily — these are the areas where milk and drool accumulate.
